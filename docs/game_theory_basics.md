# Game Theory Basics

This document covers fundamental concepts in game theory.

## 1. Nash Equilibrium
A strategy profile where no player has an incentive to unilaterally deviate.
- **Pure Strategy:** A deterministic choice of an action.
- **Mixed Strategy:** A probability distribution over possible actions.

## 2. Dominant Strategies
A strategy is dominant if it yields a higher payoff than any other strategy, regardless of what the other players do.
- **Strictly Dominant:** Payoff is strictly greater.
- **Weakly Dominant:** Payoff is greater than or equal to.

## 3. Types of Games
- **Zero-Sum Games:** One player's gain is exactly another player's loss.
- **Cooperative Games:** Players can form binding agreements.
- **Non-Cooperative Games:** Players cannot form binding agreements; interactions are self-enforcing.
- **Simultaneous Games:** Players act at the same time without knowing others' actions.
- **Sequential Games:** Players take turns and can observe previous actions.
